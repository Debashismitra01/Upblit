Community Handbook

üìö Getting Started

Setting Up Your Environment

  • Install Git and create a GitHub account
  • Fork the repository you want to contribute to
  • Clone your fork locally
  • Set up the development environment as per project README

Making Your First Contribution

  • Look for issues labeled "good first issue"
  • Comment on the issue to let others know you're working on it
  • Create a new branch for your changes
  • Make your changes and commit with clear messages
  • Push your changes and create a pull request

üí° Best Practices

  • Write clean, readable code with proper comments
  • Follow the project's coding standards and style guide
  • Test your changes thoroughly before submitting
  • Write descriptive commit messages
  • Keep pull requests focused and small
  • Respond to feedback constructively
  • Update documentation when necessary

ü§ù Community Guidelines

  • Be respectful and inclusive in all interactions
  • Help newcomers feel welcome
  • Share knowledge and learn from others
  • Give credit where credit is due
  • Report issues and bugs you encounter
  • Participate in discussions and community events

Code of Conduct

Our commitment to fostering an inclusive and respectful community

ü§ù Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

‚≠ê Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

  • Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
  • Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
  • Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
  • Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes
  • Focusing on what is best for the overall community

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

  • The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
  • Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
  • Public or private harassment
  • Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
  • Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

üì¢ Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

üéÉ Hacktoberfest

Join us for the biggest open source celebration of the year!

üåü What is Hacktoberfest?

Hacktoberfest is a month-long celebration of open source software run by DigitalOcean. It's open to everyone in our global community! Whether you're a developer, student learning to code, event host, or company of any size, you can help drive growth of open source and make positive contributions to an ever-growing community.

üéØ How to Participate

  • Register: Sign up on the official Hacktoberfest website between September 26 and October 31
  • Contribute: Submit 4 quality pull requests to participating repositories during October
  • Win: Complete the challenge and receive exclusive digital rewards or plant a tree
  • Learn: Engage with the community, learn new skills, and make meaningful contributions

üìã Our Participating Projects

We have several repositories participating in Hacktoberfest! Look for issues labeled with "hacktoberfest" to find contribution opportunities.

  • Web Development Starter Kits
  • Community Documentation Portal
  • Open Source Learning Platform
  • Developer Tools Collection
  • API Examples Repository

üèÜ Quality Standards

To ensure quality contributions, please follow these guidelines:

  • Read the project's contributing guidelines carefully
  • Choose issues that match your skill level
  • Write clean, well-documented code
  • Test your changes thoroughly
  • Submit meaningful pull requests (no spam)
  • Be patient and responsive to feedback
